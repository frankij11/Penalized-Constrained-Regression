Introduction

Learning curves are fundamental to cost estimation in manufacturing and aerospace industries. The classic power-law model describes how unit costs decrease with cumulative production:

\[Y = T_1 \cdot X^b\]

where \(Y\) is the unit cost, \(T_1\) is the theoretical first unit cost, \(X\) is the cumulative unit number, and \(b\) is the learning curve slope (typically negative, indicating cost reduction with experience).

When multiple factors affect costs (e.g., production rate effects), the model extends to:

\[Y = T_1 \cdot X_1^b \cdot X_2^c\]

where \(X_1\) represents cumulative midpoint and \(X_2\) represents lot quantity (rate variable).

The Challenge with OLS

Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression on log-transformed data is the traditional approach:

\[\log(Y) = \log(T_1) + b \cdot \log(X_1) + c \cdot \log(X_2)\]

However, OLS can produce problematic results:

  1. Wrong signs: Coefficients may have positive signs when domain knowledge requires negative values
  2. Multicollinearity: Correlated predictors inflate variance and produce unstable estimates
  3. Small samples: Limited data in early production phases leads to poor estimates

Our Approach: Penalized-Constrained Regression

This paper introduces Penalized-Constrained Regression (PCReg), which combines:

  • Elastic net regularization: Shrinkage to reduce variance and handle multicollinearity
  • Bound constraints: Domain knowledge encoded as coefficient bounds (e.g., \(b \leq 0\), \(c \leq 0\))
  • Direct optimization: Minimize sum of squared percentage errors (SSPE) in unit space
